## Paper Rules

A paper is RELEVANT if it meets ANY of the following:
- Directly studies consumer behavior, decision-making, or psychology in marketing contexts
- Examines technology's impact on consumers (AI, social media, digital platforms, e-commerce)
- Introduces methodological innovations applicable to marketing research
- Studies branding, advertising effectiveness, or persuasion
- Covers market dynamics, pricing, or competitive strategy with behavioral implications

A paper is NOT RELEVANT if:
- It is purely operations research, supply chain, or logistics with no consumer angle
- It is a purely statistical/econometric methods paper with no marketing application
- It covers organizational behavior or HR topics
- It is an editorial, erratum, or table of contents

## Post Rules

A post is RELEVANT if it meets ANY of the following:
- Discusses new research findings in consumer behavior, marketing, or related fields
- Shares insights about technology trends affecting consumers or marketers
- Links to or comments on relevant academic papers
- Provides professional commentary on marketing industry developments
- Discusses research methodology debates relevant to marketing

A post is NOT RELEVANT if:
- It is purely personal content (vacation photos, food, etc.)
- It is about unrelated academic fields
- It is a simple repost/share with no commentary
- It is purely promotional or spam
